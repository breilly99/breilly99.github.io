About Me

Connecticut

I grew up in Manchester, Connecticut, a small town outside of Hartford. I lived there for 14 years before moving.

I always grew up playing sports. Since I was three, I played baseball, basketball, and swam as well. Sports have always been a huge part of my life.

I am a big Boston sports fan, whether it is the Patriots, Red Sox, Celtics or Bruins, I grew up loving Boston sports.

With my dad, we would often make the trip from Manchester to Boston to go to games, only adding to my love of sports more.

Going to games, or watching them on television or listening on radio, made me start to think how could I stay in sports.

When I was 14, my dad's job made my family move from Manchester to Charlotte, North Carolina, saying goodbye to all my friends and family in the Northeast.

Charlotte

After moving to Charlotte, I continued to play sports and also started playing football in high school. At my new school, Charlotte Latin, they offered a journalism class, and this is where I got my start.

My school offered a journalism class that I took for three years, setting the ground floor for what my future would hold. I would make packages, write stories, and cover games for my class.

While in high school, I wanted to go to a school that had a strong journalism program. I applied only to schools that would give me an opportunity to broadcast, one of those being the University of Miami.

After taking my tour, I knew that Miami was the place I wanted to be after hearing about UMTV and WVUM 90.5FM, and the student broadcasting opportunities that were available.

The University of Miami

When I came to the University of Miami, I immediatley joined UMTV and WVUM 90.5FM my freshman year. I started off as an analyst on UMTV and then a broadcaster on smaller games for WVUM.

In my second semester, I started to make a jump at both places. I was named an anchor for UMTV's SportsDesk as a freshman and was able to go on Women's Basketball road trips.

I ended up calling over 30 games as a freshman for WVUM, setting myself up to be named Sports Director as a sophomore, and I have held the position for the past two and a half years.

Once I took over as Sports Director, I took over a staff of over 20 people, working closely with Miami Athletics and scheduling all broadcasts for each week.

WVUM has taken me places I could have never imagined, including stadiums such as Cameron Indoor Stadium, the Carrier Dome, and Dean Smith Arena.

Also, this past semester, I was able to travel for the Men's and Women's NCAA Tournaments, broadcasting solo on WVUM 90.5FM.

In total, I have broadcasted over 150 games during my time at Miami, taking up a huge portion of my time in college, but one that I am forever grateful for.

The Future

This upcoming summer, I will be broadcasting for the Cowlitz Black Bears of the West Coast League.

I was trying to enter Minor League Baseball this summer, but with start dates not lining up with my graduation, I decided to go back to a collegiate summer league to gain more experience.

I am actively looking for play-by-play jobs at colleges for the fall and winter hoping to become the voice of a university.
